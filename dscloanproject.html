1. Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)

A Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is an electronic form of a physical signature. It is mandatory for filing income tax returns, MCA forms, GST registrations, e-tendering, and various other government portal submissions. We assist in obtaining Class 3 DSC for individuals and organisations.

Who Needs a DSC?

  • Directors and Authorised Signatories of Companies and LLPs (mandatory for MCA filings)
  • Individuals and businesses filing Income Tax Returns with audit reports
  • GST-registered taxpayers for certain filings and refund claims
  • Contractors and vendors participating in e-tendering on government portals
  • Importers and exporters using the ICEGATE customs portal
  • Professionals such as CA, CS, and Cost Accountants for statutory filings

Documents Required for DSC

  • PAN Card (mandatory)
  • Aadhaar Card (for Aadhaar-based eKYC verification)
  • Passport-size photograph
  • Mobile number linked to Aadhaar (for OTP verification)
  • Organisation registration certificate (for company/LLP DSC)
💡 Quick Tip: Class 3 DSC is required for most MCA and income tax filings. It is issued with a validity of 1 or 2 years and can be renewed before expiry. We handle the complete process — from application to delivery of the USB token.

2. Loan Appraisal Services

A Loan Appraisal Report is a detailed assessment of a borrower's financial position, repayment capacity, and creditworthiness — prepared for submission to banks and financial institutions. Our appraisal reports are structured to meet the specific requirements of PSU banks, cooperative banks, and NBFCs.

Types of Loans We Assist With

  • Business Loans and Working Capital Loans (CC / OD limits)
  • Term Loans for purchase of machinery, equipment, or property
  • MSME Loans under government schemes (MUDRA, CGTMSE, Stand-Up India)
  • Home Loans and Mortgage Loans
  • Education Loans
  • Project Finance for new or expansion businesses

What Our Loan Appraisal Covers

  • Detailed analysis of Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last 3 years
  • Profit & Loss Account and Balance Sheet preparation or review
  • Calculation of DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) and other financial ratios
  • Assessment of business turnover, profitability, and net worth
  • Preparation of CMA (Credit Monitoring Arrangement) Data as required by banks
  • Coordination with bank officials for a smooth sanction process

3. Financial Project Report

A Financial Project Report (also called a Detailed Project Report or DPR) is a comprehensive document that outlines the technical, financial, and commercial viability of a proposed business or expansion project. It is mandatory for bank loan applications, government scheme approvals, and investor presentations.

Who Needs a Project Report?

  • New entrepreneurs applying for a business loan or MUDRA loan
  • Existing businesses seeking term loans for expansion or modernisation
  • Startups applying for government grants or subsidies
  • Businesses applying under PM Vishwakarma, PMEGP, WBSEDCL, or other state schemes
  • Professionals setting up clinics, studios, or service centres

What a Project Report Includes

  1. Executive Summary: Overview of the proposed business, promoter background, and funding requirement.
  2. Business Description: Nature of business, products/services, target market, and competitive landscape.
  3. Technical Details: Location, infrastructure, machinery, raw materials, manpower plan, and production capacity.
  4. Market Analysis: Demand assessment, pricing strategy, and marketing plan.
  5. Financial Projections: Projected Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow Statements, Break-Even Analysis, and IRR/NPV calculations for 5 years.
  6. Loan Requirement & Means of Finance: Details of own contribution, bank loan amount, and utilisation plan.
  7. Repayment Schedule: Projected EMI/instalment schedule showing repayment capacity to the bank.
  8. Risk Analysis & Mitigation: Identification of key business risks and mitigation strategies.
💡 Important: Banks and government bodies have specific formats for Project Reports. Our team prepares reports in compliance with SBI, UCO Bank, Bank of Baroda, PNB, and West Bengal state government formats — improving your chances of fast approval.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get a DSC?

A Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is typically issued within 1 to 3 working days after successful eKYC verification via Aadhaar OTP. Physical token delivery may take an additional 1–2 days depending on your location in Howrah or West Bengal.

What is CMA Data and why do banks require it?

CMA (Credit Monitoring Arrangement) Data is a standardised financial statement format prescribed by the RBI and used by banks to assess a borrower's past performance and future projections. It includes the last 3 years' audited financials and 5 years' projected financials. Most banks require CMA Data for working capital loans above ₹10 lakh and all term loans.

Can you prepare a project report for a MUDRA loan?

Yes. We prepare project reports specifically for MUDRA loans (Shishu, Kishore, and Tarun categories) as well as PMEGP, PM Vishwakarma, and other government-backed schemes. Our reports are aligned with the bank's requirements and include all financial projections required for sanction.

Do I need a CA for a project report or loan appraisal?

While it is not always mandatory, a project report or loan appraisal prepared or reviewed by a qualified Chartered Accountant carries significantly more weight with banks and financial institutions. Our team includes qualified CA professionals who review and certify all financial documents before submission.

What is the cost of preparing a project report?

The cost depends on the complexity of the project, the loan amount, and the bank's specific requirements. We offer competitive and transparent pricing with no hidden charges. Contact our Howrah or Kolkata office for a customised quote based on your specific requirement.
