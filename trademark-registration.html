Trademark Registration in Kolkata & Howrah

What is Trademark Registration?

A trademark is a unique sign, symbol, word, phrase, logo, or combination thereof that distinguishes your goods or services from those of other businesses. Trademark registration gives your brand exclusive legal rights under the Trade Marks Act, 1999, and prevents others from using a similar mark that could cause confusion in the market.

Once registered, a trademark is valid for 10 years and can be renewed indefinitely every 10 years. It is one of the most valuable intellectual property (IP) assets a business can own — protecting your brand reputation, customer trust, and commercial goodwill.

💡 Did you know? You can use the ™ symbol immediately after filing a trademark application. The ® symbol (Registered Trademark) can only be used once the trademark is officially registered by the Trademark Registry of India.

Who Should Register a Trademark?

  • Startup founders and entrepreneurs who want to protect their brand from day one
  • Small and medium businesses (SMEs) looking to build a recognisable market identity
  • E-commerce sellers on Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho, and other platforms
  • Manufacturers, traders, and service providers using a unique brand name or logo
  • Professionals such as consultants, doctors, and educators offering services under a brand
  • Individuals, companies, LLPs, and partnership firms — anyone can apply for a trademark

Documents Required

  • PAN Card of the applicant (individual, proprietor, or authorised signatory)
  • Aadhaar Card or any valid government-issued identity proof
  • Clear image of the trademark / logo in JPEG format (if it's a device/logo mark)
  • Business registration certificate (if applicable — GST certificate, Shop Act, etc.)
  • Udyam Registration Certificate (for MSME — concessional government fee applies)
  • Signed Power of Attorney (Form TM-48) authorising us to file on your behalf
  • Description of goods or services for which the trademark will be used

Our Process

  1. Trademark Search: We conduct a thorough search on the IP India portal to check if an identical or similar mark already exists — reducing the risk of objections or oppositions.
  2. Class Identification: We identify the correct trademark class (out of 45 Nice Classification classes) based on your products or services to ensure maximum coverage.
  3. Application Drafting: We prepare and review your trademark application (Form TM-A) with accurate details and attach the required documents.
  4. Filing on IP India Portal: The application is filed online on the official IP India portal. An acknowledgement receipt with the application number is generated immediately.
  5. Vienna Codification (for logo marks): If your trademark includes a logo or device, the Trademark Office assigns Vienna codes to categorise the figurative elements.
  6. Examination & Response: The Trademark Office examines the application and may issue an Examination Report. We draft and file a professional reply if any objection is raised.
  7. Publication in Trademark Journal: Upon acceptance, the mark is advertised in the weekly Trademark Journal for 4 months, during which third parties may oppose the registration.
  8. Registration Certificate: If no opposition is filed (or opposition is decided in your favour), the trademark is registered and a Registration Certificate is issued, valid for 10 years.

Benefits of Registering Your Trademark

  • Exclusive nationwide legal right to use the mark for the registered class of goods/services
  • Right to sue infringers and claim damages in a court of law
  • Use of the ® symbol, which signals authenticity and boosts customer confidence
  • Protection against counterfeit products and brand misuse
  • Trademark can be licensed or franchised, creating an additional revenue stream
  • Treated as an intangible business asset — adds to company valuation during investment or M&A
  • Brand protection on e-commerce platforms (Amazon Brand Registry, Flipkart, etc.)
  • Valid across India with international filing options available under the Madrid Protocol

Government Fee Structure

  • Individual / Startup / MSME (with Udyam certificate): ₹4,500 per class per application
  • Company / LLP / Partnership Firm / Other Entities: ₹9,000 per class per application
  • Above fees are for online filing. Physical filing attracts a higher fee.
  • Our professional service charges are separate and transparent — no hidden costs.
💡 Tip: If your business qualifies as an MSME under the Udyam Registration scheme, you pay only 50% of the standard government trademark fee — a significant saving. Ask our team to check your eligibility before filing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does trademark registration take in India?

The overall process — from filing to receiving the registration certificate — typically takes 18 to 24 months, depending on whether any objection or opposition arises. However, you receive a filing acknowledgement immediately, and the ™ symbol can be used right after filing. In cases without objections, the process can sometimes be faster.

Can I register a trademark before my business is formally registered?

Yes. Any individual, including a sole proprietor or a person intending to start a business, can apply for trademark registration in their own name. You do not need a company or LLP registration to file a trademark application. This is actually advisable — registering your trademark early protects your brand name before someone else claims it.

What happens if my trademark application receives an objection?

The Trademark Registrar issues an Examination Report stating the grounds of objection. You (or your agent) must file a written reply within 30 days. If the response is satisfactory, the application proceeds to advertisement in the Trademark Journal. Our team prepares a detailed legal response to overcome objections and maximise the chances of acceptance.

Can a trademark be transferred or sold?

Yes. A registered trademark is a transferable property right. It can be assigned (sold), licensed, or mortgaged just like any other business asset. Assignments can be with or without the goodwill of the business, and must be recorded with the Trademark Registry to be legally effective.

What if someone is already using a similar name but hasn't registered it?

In India, rights in a trademark can arise through use (common law rights) even without registration. However, an unregistered mark is much harder to enforce and gives you limited legal recourse. If you register first, you generally have a stronger legal standing. Our preliminary trademark search will flag any prior users so we can advise you accordingly before filing.
