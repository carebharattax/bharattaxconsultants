What is Stock Market & Commodity Investment?

The stock market allows individuals and institutions to buy and sell shares of publicly listed companies on exchanges such as the NSE (National Stock Exchange) and BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange). Investing in equities over the long term has historically been one of the most effective ways to grow wealth and beat inflation in India.

Commodity markets, on the other hand, allow trading in physical goods such as gold, silver, crude oil, natural gas and agricultural products through exchanges like MCX (Multi Commodity Exchange) and NCDEX (National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange). Commodities serve as an excellent diversifier in a balanced investment portfolio.

Who Should Invest in the Stock Market?

  • Salaried individuals looking to grow wealth beyond fixed deposits and savings accounts
  • Business owners seeking to deploy surplus funds productively in equity markets
  • Young investors with a long time horizon of 7 to 10 years or more for equity investing
  • Investors seeking inflation-beating returns over the medium to long term
  • Those looking to diversify their existing portfolio beyond mutual funds and real estate
  • Retirees seeking dividend income from blue-chip stocks with stable track records
  • Anyone interested in applying for upcoming IPOs of high-growth companies

Tax on Stock Market & Commodity Gains

  • Equity STCG (held < 12 months): Taxed at 20% under Section 111A
  • Equity LTCG (held > 12 months): Gains above ₹1.25 lakh taxed at 12.5% — no indexation benefit
  • Intraday Trading: Treated as speculative business income — taxed at your applicable income slab rate
  • F&O Trading: Treated as non-speculative business income — taxed at slab rate; audit may be required
  • Commodity Gains: Taxed as business income or capital gains depending on nature and frequency of trades
  • STT (Securities Transaction Tax): Automatically deducted on equity trades — relevant for capital gains calculation
💡 Note: If you are an active trader in F&O or intraday segments, you are required to file ITR-3 and may need a tax audit under Section 44AB if your turnover exceeds the prescribed limit. We handle your complete tax filing including trading income.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I open a demat account to invest in the stock market?

You need a demat account and a trading account with a SEBI-registered broker. The process requires PAN, Aadhaar, bank details and a photograph for KYC. Most brokers complete the process online within 24 to 48 hours. We guide you in choosing the right broker and completing the account opening smoothly.

What is the difference between NSE and BSE?

NSE (National Stock Exchange) and BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange) are India's two primary stock exchanges. NSE is the larger exchange by trading volume and is home to the Nifty 50 index. BSE is the older exchange and tracks the Sensex index. Most large-cap stocks are listed and actively traded on both exchanges.

How are stock market gains taxed in India?

Short-term capital gains on listed equity (held less than 12 months) are taxed at 20%. Long-term capital gains above ₹1.25 lakh (held more than 12 months) are taxed at 12.5%. Intraday and F&O gains are treated as business income and taxed at your income slab rate. We handle all capital gains reporting in your ITR.

What is an IPO and how can I apply for one?

An IPO (Initial Public Offering) is when a company lists its shares on the stock exchange for the first time. You can apply through your demat account using the ASBA facility via your bank or UPI payment. We track all upcoming IPOs, evaluate their subscription worthiness and guide you on lot size, price band and allotment process.
